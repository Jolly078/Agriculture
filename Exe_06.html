AGRICULTURE

Agriculture is the science, art, and practice of cultivating plants and livestock. It includes the production of food, fiber, medicinal plants, and other products that are essential for human survival and economic stability.

Main Branches of Agriculture

  1. Crop Production
  2. Animal Husbandry
  3. Horticulture
  4. Forestry
  5. Aquaculture

Explanation

  1. Crop Prosuction:Involves the cultivation of plants for food, fiber, biofuel, and other purposes. This includes cereals (like wheat and rice), fruits, vegetables, and other crops.
  2. Animal Husbandry: The practice of breeding and raising livestock such as cattle, sheep, goats, pigs, and poultry for meat, milk, eggs, and other products.
  3. Horticulture: Focuses on the cultivation of fruits, vegetables, flowers, and ornamental plants. It also includes landscape gardening and greenhouse farming.
  4. Forestry: The management and cultivation of forests for wood, fuel, and other products, as well as for conservation and recreational purposes.
  5. Aquaculture: The farming of fish, crustaceans, mollusks, aquatic plants, and other aquatic organisms for food and other products.
